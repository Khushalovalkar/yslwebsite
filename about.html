Our History

Yves Saint Laurent (YSL) is one of the most iconic fashion houses, known for its groundbreaking designs and influence on modern fashion. Here's a brief history of YSL: Founding and Early Years 1961: Yves Saint Laurent and his partner Pierre Bergé founded the Yves Saint Laurent fashion house in Paris. Saint Laurent had already made a name for himself as the head designer at Dior, where he succeeded Christian Dior at the age of 21. The first YSL collection debuted in 1962, showcasing Saint Laurent's innovative approach to blending art, culture, and fashion. Innovations and Contributions to Fashion Le Smoking (1966): YSL introduced the first women's tuxedo suit, a bold move that challenged gender norms and redefined power dressing for women. Ready-to-Wear (1966): YSL launched "Rive Gauche," a ready-to-wear line, making high fashion more accessible and marking a shift in the industry. Cultural Inspirations: YSL was inspired by global cultures, art movements (such as Pop Art and Cubism), and artists like Mondrian, resulting in iconic collections like the Mondrian dresses (1965). 1970s and 1980s: Dominance in Fashion YSL continued to revolutionize fashion by introducing safari jackets, sheer blouses, and peasant dresses. His work reflected the liberation of women and a shift toward more relaxed yet elegant styles. YSL became synonymous with the jet-set lifestyle, appealing to celebrities, royals, and art enthusiasts worldwide. Later Years and Legacy In the 1980s and 1990s, the brand remained a dominant force in fashion, even as Yves Saint Laurent himself faced health challenges. Yves Saint Laurent retired in 2002, and his final couture show was a tribute to his illustrious career. Yves passed away in 2008, leaving behind an unmatched legacy in the fashion world. Modern Era In 1999, Gucci Group (now Kering) acquired YSL. Tom Ford initially designed the ready-to-wear collections, bringing a sleek and provocative aesthetic to the brand. Since then, several creative directors, including Stefano Pilati, Hedi Slimane, and Anthony Vaccarello (current as of 2024), have led the brand, each leaving their mark. Key Contributions Empowering Women: Redefined women's fashion with a mix of masculinity and elegance. Cultural Impact: YSL blurred the lines between fashion and art, making bold cultural statements. Global Influence: Established Paris as the heart of high fashion and inspired countless designers. YSL remains a symbol of innovation, elegance, and timeless style in the fashion world.

Our Mission

The mission of Yves Saint Laurent (YSL) is centered on maintaining its legacy as a pioneer in the fashion world while continuously innovating and redefining modern luxury. The brand seeks to create designs that are bold, empowering, and inclusive, reflecting the spirit of its founder Yves Saint Laurent, who championed creativity and breaking societal norms.

Our Values

The value of Yves Saint Laurent (YSL) lies in its reputation as a pioneering luxury brand that blends innovation, elegance, and cultural relevance.
